Personal Profile

Nitinjeet Sahu (born 22 September 2013) is an Indian student, young author, and world record holder, known for his early contributions to motivational writing and student-focused literature. He is the author of The Last Exam, a motivational book aimed at students and competitive examination aspirants. He has received recognition from national and international literary and record-keeping institutions for his work at a young age.

Early Life and Education

Nitinjeet Sahu was born in Bhadrak district, Odisha, India. He is currently pursuing his school education at SBD International School, Bhadrak. From an early age, he showed an interest in reading, writing, and academic learning, particularly in subjects related to social sciences, general knowledge, and self-development.

Alongside formal education, he has consistently engaged in independent reading and writing, focusing on building conceptual clarity, discipline, and long-term academic habits.

Writing Career

Nitinjeet Sahu began writing at a young age, initially as a means of self-expression and structured thinking. His interest gradually developed into purposeful writing, culminating in the publication of his motivational book, The Last Exam.

The Last Exam addresses themes such as exam pressure, self-doubt, discipline, consistency, and mental resilience among students. It is intended to encourage learners to adopt a balanced and disciplined approach toward education, emphasizing effort and character over short-term results.

Achievements and Recognition

For his literary contribution, Nitinjeet Sahu has been recognized by multiple organizations. He has been listed as a record holder by the International Book of Records and has received awards and felicitations from various cultural, educational, and literary platforms, including district-level and national forums.

He has also been honored at cultural and literary events for promoting reading and writing among students.

Interests and Aspirations

Beyond writing, Nitinjeet Sahu is interested in academics, public affairs, and structured learning. He aspires to serve in public administration and is preparing himself with a long-term focus on discipline, foundational knowledge, and ethical values.

Personal Life

He is the son of Bhaskar Chandra Sahu and Rachita Sahu. He resides in Bhadrak, Odisha.

Philosophy

Nitinjeet Sahu believes that meaningful achievement is built through consistency, humility, and continuous learning. He views recognition as a responsibility rather than an endpoint and emphasizes steady progress over immediate validation.
